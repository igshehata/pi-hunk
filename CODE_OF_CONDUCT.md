# Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

We pledge to make participation in pi-hunk welcoming and harassment-free for everyone, regardless of
age, body size, disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of
experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or
sexual identity and orientation.

## Expected behavior

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive community include:

- showing empathy and respect for different viewpoints and experiences;
- giving and accepting constructive, specific feedback;
- focusing discussion on the technical problem rather than the person;
- taking responsibility, apologizing, and learning from mistakes;
- prioritizing the health of the community over individual preferences.

Unacceptable behavior includes harassment, insults, threats, sexualized attention, deliberate
intimidation, publishing private information, or sustained disruption of project spaces.

## Enforcement

Project maintainers may edit, reject, or remove contributions and may temporarily or permanently ban
participants whose behavior is inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful. Enforcement
reports should be made privately through the contact route in [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md), not in a
public issue. Reports will be reviewed promptly and handled with discretion.

## Scope and attribution

This code applies in project spaces and when someone publicly represents the project. It is adapted
from the
[Contributor Covenant, version 2.1](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct.html).
